|Term - "Tabular Data Generation"
|------------------------------>
    The process of creating artificial datasets that mimic statistcal
    properties of real data while preserving privacy.

|Term - "Structural Causal Model"
|------------------------------>
    Different from the "Rubin Causal Model". Focusses on a single mech that
    generates data, allowing for direct modelling of cuasal relationships
    through equations.

    Instead of only a DAG to represent the model, equations are used alongside
    it.
 

|Term - "Global Utility"
|------------------------------>
    Metric proposed in TabStruct paper to evaulate the casual structural
    integrity of the generated dataset.

    Does not require a Structural Causal Model (SCM) to measure the
    preservation of causality.

|Term - "Markov Property"
|------------------------------>
    The Markov Property is the memoryless property of a stochastic process,
    which means that its future evolution is independent of its history. The
    Markov assumption is used to describe a model where the markob property is
    assumed to hole.

|Term - "Stochastic Process"
|------------------------------>
    A mathematical object usually defined as a family of random variabnles in a
    probability space, where the index of the family often has the
    interpretation of time. i.e. growth of bacterial population, electrical
    current fluctuating due to thermal noise, or the movement of a gas
    molecule.

|Term - "Ancestral Graph"
|------------------------------>
    A mixed graph used to provide a graphical representaion for the result of
    marginalizing one or more verticies in a graphcial model that takes the
    form of a DAG.
        
        - Contain 3 types of edges:
            - Directed Edges   ( --> )
            - BiDirected Edges ( <-> )
            - UnDirected Edges ( --- )
        - Has 3 Constraints:
            - There are no directed cycles
            - If there is an edge from a vertex u  to another vertex v , with
              an arrowhead at v v} (that is, either a directed edge from u
              {\displaystyle u} to v {\displaystyle v} or a bidirected edge),
              then there does not exist a path from v {\displaystyle v} to u
              {\displaystyle u} consisting of undirected edges and/or directed
              edges oriented consistently with the path.
            - If a vertex is an endpoint fo an undirected edge then it is not
              also the endpoint of an edge with an arrowhead at v
 

|Term - "Markov Equivilance Class"

"Prior work (Hansen et al., 2023; Qian et al., 2024; Du & Li, 2024; Tu et al.,
2024; Livieris et al., 2024; Kapar et al., 2025) has attempted to assess
tabular data generators by evaluating the synthetic data they produce." (i)
Insufficient tabular-specific fidelity assessments

"Structural fidelity, as quantified by the proposed global utility, should be a
core dimension when evaluating tabular generative models."

"An extensive line of benchmarks (Stoian et al., 2025; Hansen et al., 2023;
Qian et al., 2024; Du & Li, 2024; Kindji et al., 2024; Sidorenko et al., 2025;
Long et al., 2025) has been proposed for tabular data generation,
conventionally established around three dimensions: density estimation, privacy
preservation, and ML efficacy."

"Pearl's backdoor criterion is essentially a special-purpose, efficient search
for one particular kind of d-separation statement (one that specifically
isolates a causal effect from confounding). TabStruct's CglobalCglobal​ is
the *brute-force superset* — it doesn't care about causal effects at all, only
about fully specifying which independence statements the graph entails, so that
those statements can later be tested against real or synthetic data. You could
say Pearl's algorithm answers "*what do I need to condition on to get an
unbiased effect estimate?*" while CglobalCglobal​ answers "what does this
graph claim about independence, everywhere, exhaustively?" — the latter being
far too expensive to compute for the sole purpose of adjustment-set selection,
but exactly what's needed to check whether a synthetic dataset's joint
distribution is faithful to the true causal graph.

That's also why TabStruct restricts itself to the CPDAG level (Section D.2 in
the paper) rather than requiring full DAG orientation — full backdoor-style
reasoning needs directional information, but CI-statement matching at the CPDAG
level only needs the skeleton + immoralities, which is more tractable and still
meaningful for fidelity checking."
